# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Process Owner

### Role Summary
Process Owners ensure the ongoing health, evolution, and adoption of process documentation and practices across OctoAcme. They act as stewards of institutional knowledge and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain accountability for the overarching health of process docs and practices
- Ensure periodic reviews and updates to process documentation
- Gather team feedback on process effectiveness and pain points
- Manage process improvements and evolution
- Act as the bridge between process documentation and day-to-day practice
- Facilitate adoption of new or updated processes

### Goals
- Keep process documentation current and relevant
- Drive continuous improvement based on team feedback
- Ensure processes scale with organizational growth
- Maintain high process adoption and consistency across teams

### Typical Communication
- Monthly process review sessions with team leads
- Quarterly feedback surveys and analysis
- Process improvement proposals and decision logs
- Updates to documentation repositories and wikis

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Provides guidance on process adherence and improvement opportunities
- **Developers**: Gathers feedback on development process effectiveness
- **Product Managers**: Aligns process documentation with product delivery needs
- **All roles**: Acts as primary contact for process questions and clarifications

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders bring requirements, priorities, and strategic feedback to projects. They review deliverables, provide sign-off for major milestones, and ensure alignment with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate business requirements and priorities
- Provide timely feedback on deliverables and milestones
- Review and approve major project decisions and scope changes
- Escalate issues that impact business objectives
- Sign off on milestone completions and releases
- Ensure project outcomes align with organizational strategy

### Goals
- Maximize business value from project investments
- Ensure projects meet organizational needs and constraints
- Maintain visibility into project progress and risks
- Support timely decision-making and issue resolution

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and demo sessions
- Weekly or bi-weekly status updates
- Escalation notifications for critical issues
- Sign-off requests for major deliverables

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Primary point of contact for status, escalations, and approvals
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on requirements definition and prioritization
- **Sponsors/Leadership**: Provides business context and strategic alignment
- **Communication Lead**: Receives regular updates and participates in stakeholder briefings

---

## Communication Lead

### Role Summary
Communication Leads maintain transparency and ensure timely, effective communication across the team and stakeholders. They own communication channels, regular check-ins, status reporting, and escalation communications.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain communication channels and cadence
- Coordinate regular team check-ins and status updates
- Ensure timely and transparent stakeholder communications
- Manage escalation communications and incident notifications
- Standardize communication templates and formats
- Track and report on communication effectiveness

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders receive timely, relevant information
- Prevent communication gaps and information silos
- Maintain transparency about project status and risks
- Facilitate effective collaboration across distributed teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups facilitation or coordination
- Weekly status reports and stakeholder briefings
- Monthly communication effectiveness reviews
- Ad-hoc escalation and incident notifications

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates closely on status reporting and stakeholder management
- **Risk Manager**: Partners on risk escalations and mitigation communications
- **Stakeholders**: Primary contact for regular updates and milestone communications
- **All team members**: Ensures everyone has access to necessary information

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers proactively identify, assess, track, and mitigate project risks. They lead risk escalation paths, develop mitigation plans, and ensure the team maintains awareness of potential issues.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and assess project risks throughout the lifecycle
- Maintain and update the risk register
- Develop and track mitigation plans and contingencies
- Lead risk escalation processes
- Facilitate risk assessment sessions
- Monitor risk indicators and early warning signals
- Coordinate risk responses across the team

### Goals
- Minimize negative impact of risks on project success
- Maintain proactive rather than reactive risk management
- Ensure timely escalation of critical risks
- Build risk awareness across the project team

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk register updates
- Risk assessment sessions during planning
- Escalation notifications for high-impact risks
- Monthly risk trend analysis and reporting

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on risk identification and mitigation planning
- **Communication Lead**: Partners on risk escalations and stakeholder notifications
- **Developers**: Works with technical team to identify and mitigate technical risks
- **Product Manager**: Aligns risk mitigation with product priorities and trade-offs
- **All delivery roles**: Gathers input on emerging risks and mitigation effectiveness

---

## Change Champion

### Role Summary
Change Champions advocate for new practices, tools, and cultural improvements. They facilitate adoption of changes, train team members, monitor feedback, and ensure successful change management across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Identify opportunities for process, tool, or cultural improvements
- Build support and advocacy for proposed changes
- Develop and execute change management plans
- Train and support team members through transitions
- Monitor adoption metrics and gather feedback
- Address resistance and facilitate smooth transitions
- Celebrate successes and share lessons learned

### Goals
- Drive successful adoption of beneficial changes
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Build organizational change capability
- Ensure changes deliver intended improvements

### Typical Communication
- Change proposals and business cases
- Training sessions and workshops
- Adoption metrics and feedback summaries
- Success stories and lessons learned

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Process Owner**: Partners on process improvement initiatives
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates change rollout with project timelines
- **Team Leads**: Works through team leads to facilitate adoption
- **All team members**: Provides training, support, and addresses concerns
